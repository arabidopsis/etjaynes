Jaynes' Articles from http://bayes.wustl.edu/

  • Bayesian Method: General Background. presented at the 4th anual Workshop on Baysean/Maximum Entropy Methods. Calgary 1984
  • Evolution of Carnot's Principle. presented at EMBO workshop on Maximum-Entropy in x-ray crystallography Orsay 1984.
  • Inferential Scattering. "Generalized Scattering" at the Second Maximum Entropy Workshop Laramie WY, 1982. in Maximum-Entropy and Bayesian Methods in Inverse Problems, C.R. Smith & W.T. Grandy eds. (1985). Revised and extended here 1993. -- propagation of inferences is just like Quantum scattering theory.
  • Bayesian Spectrum and Chirp Analysis. Revised and updated paper presented at the Third Workshop on Maximum-Entropy and Bayesian Methods, Laramie Wyoming, Aug 1983. Published in Maximum-Entropy and Spectral Analysis and Estimation Problems, C.R. Smith and G.J. Erickson, Eds. 1987. Assumes spectrum of the form cos(wt + at^2 + theta). See the Bretthorst Lecture notes here (1,2,3,4).
  • Probability Theory as Logic. Presented at the 9th Annual Workshop on Maximum Entropy and Bayesian Methods, Dartmouth Colledge, New Hampshire, Aug 1989. In Maximum Entropy and Bayesian Methods, Paul F Fougere Ed. Kluwer (1990). Revised and extended 1994. The "Mind Projection Fallacy" and discusses AI.
  • Macroscopic Prediction. In Complex Systems-Operational Approaches in Neurobiology, Physics and Computers, H. Haken Ed. 1985."Maximum Caliber" Principle. "Why is it that knowledge of microphenomena does not seem sufficient to understand macrophenomena?..... A macrostate has a crucially important further property (entropy) that is not determined by the microstate".
  • Disturbing the Memory. Thoughts on Freeman Dyson's "Disturbing the Universe" 1979.
  • How Should We Use Entropy in Economics?. "half baked ideas"
  • The Gibbs Paradox. In Maximum Entropy and Bayesian Methods, C.R. Smith, G.J. Erickson, & P.O. Neudorfer, Eds. Kluwer 1992. Good discussion of second law/Maxwell's demon using two kinds on argon and "Whifnium" showing how Entropy is information.
  • Detection of Extra-Solar-System Planets. In Maximum-Entropy and Bayesian Methods in Science and Engineering, Vol 1 G.J. Erickson & C.R. Smith eds. Kluwer 1988. Using some of Bretthorst work. (Links given above)
  • Monkeys, Kangaroos, and N. Presented at 4th Annual Workshop on Bayesian/Maximum Entropy Methods, Calgary 1984. In the Proceedings Volume, Maximum Entropy and Bayesian Methods in Applied Statistics, J Justice, Ed. Cam. Univ. Press 1986. Revised 1994. Good one for the effect of prior on the final probability.
  • Clearing up the Mysteries-- The Original Goal. Opening talk at the 8th Int. MAXENT Workshop, St John's College Cambridge England 1988. In the Proceedings Volume, Maximum Entropy and Bayesian Methods, J Skilling Ed. Kluwer 1989. Very Good description of the introduction of time asymmetry into diffusion equations and a proof of Fick's law. Bell inequalities and EPR. Bernoulli's Urn and Inference backward in time. Muscle efficiency.
  • Scattering of Light by Free Electrons. Presented at the Workshop "The Electron 1990" at St. Francis Xavier University, Antogonosh, Nova Scotia, 1990. to be published in the proceedings vol, A. Weingartshofer & D. Hestenes Eds. Jaynes tackling QM scattering theory.
  • The Theory of Radar Target Discrimination. 1989. 20 pages.
  • Probability in Quantum Theory. Revised and extended version of a paper presented at the Workshop on Complexity, Entropy and the Physics of Information, Santa Fe, New Mexico 1989. Original version in the Proceedings Volume, Complexity, Entropy and the Physics of Information, W.H. Zurek Ed. 1990. Good bit on the Lamb shift using field oscillators (page 13). Equation for friction etc. see Chandler Intro to Modern Stat Mech. Langevin equation page 260.
  • A Backward look to the Future. 1993 in Physics and Probability: Essays in honor on Edwin T Jaynes. W.T Grandy & P.W. Milonni eds Cambridge University Press. Jaynes takes on his detractors.
  • Notes on Present Status and Future Prospects. (13 pages) AI, MYCIN and probabilities.
  • Commentary on Two Articles bu C.A. Los. In Vol 3 of special issues. "On System-theoretic Methods in Economic Modelling", S. Mittnik Ed. in Computers and Mathematics with Applications.
  • The Second Law as Physical Fact and as Human Inference. (1998?) What does reversibility mean? Second law means "reproducibility" not "disorder".
  • Note on Thermal Heating Efficiency. Shows how second law + heat pump can deliver more efficient heaters! Concise (5 pages) and interesting!
  • Highly Informative Priors. Bayesian Statistics 2. pp 329-360. Bernardo, DeGroot, Lindley, Smith Eds. Elsevier Science 1985. (31 pages) An historical survey, followed by a worked-out example (seasonal adjustment in econometrics) show how much prior information can affect our final conclusions in a way that cannot even be stated in the language of orthodox statistical theory, because it does not admit the concept of correlations in a posterior distribution function. Has a discussion at the end.
